AUSTRALIA



  • Area: 7,692,024 km2
  • Population: 25.6 million
  • Capital: Canberra
  • Currency: Australian dollar (AUD)
  • Language: English
  • Fun fact: More than two thirds of the country is desert known as the ‘outback’.


Top 3 Places to Visit in Australia

1. Sydney Opera House

The Sydney Opera House is a multi-venue performing arts centre at Sydney Harbour in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. Its design features a series of large concrete “shells”. The Sydney Opera House has theatres, studios, a concert hall, exhibition rooms and a cinema. Visitors can dine at one of the restaurants, enjoy a performance or take a tour of the building.



2. Great Barrier Reef Marine Park

The Great Barrier Reef is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and it is the world's largest coral reef systems. The coral reef system has over 2,900 individual reefs and 900 islands stretching for over 2,300 kilometres. It is located in the Coral Sea, off the coast of Queensland, Australia. The Great Barrier Reef is also visible from outer space. The Great Barrier Reef Marine Park was established to protect its fragile ecosystems. The park stretches for 2,300 kilometres along Queensland. Visitors can enjoy diving, snorkelling or go on guided tours.



3. Bondi Beach

Bondi Beach is one of the world's most famous beaches. Surfing is the main sport at Bondi Beach. Visitors can stroll along the Bondi to Bronte coastal walk, which begins at the southern end of the beach and follow the coastline for six kilometres along sandstone cliffs. Visitors can also visit the nearby shops, cafes, restaurants and markets.
